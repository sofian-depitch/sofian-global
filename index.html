Global pitch deck standards

A global pitch deck follows different conventions than a Korean IR document. The standards below cover what matters most to international investors.

  • A tagline that defines what you do in one line (recognizable category in 1 second)
  • Start with context, problem, solution. Not company profile, CEO resume, or tech and patent showcase.
  • Hit the problem fast. Skip lengthy descriptions of what the audience already knows.
  • Plain language over technical jargon (investors aren't engineers)
  • Speak with certainty. "Our plan is" beats "We want to". Pick one number, not a range.
  • One slide, one message (no mixing problem, solution and traction on the same page)
  • Embed the message inside the slide title. "Path to $100M revenue" beats "Growth roadmap".
  • Big text on slides, especially for numbers. The audience should remember three numbers, not three sentences.
  • Global market strategy. A clear plan for the target region (Europe, US, SEA), not generic "global expansion".
  • Target market competitive analysis. Name the biggest competitor and beat them on one specific metric.
  • All traction on one strong slide, ordered by credibility (revenue > users > MOUs > awards)
  • Funding ask with amount, use of funds, and 12–18 month milestones
  • Build an appendix to defend Q&A. Margin, competition, clinical data, founder story stay ready without cluttering the live deck.

Will sessions be in English? Will Korean founders follow?
Yes. I have delivered 20+ seminars and 200+ 1:1 sessions in English with Korean founders, without translation issues. Working in English is also part of preparing your delegation for the global stage. International investors will not switch to Korean for them.
Why work with a non-Korean coach?
Korean startups already know how to pitch domestically. The gap is what global investors actually look for, and that is hard to see from inside Korea. I bring 13+ years across Paris, Singapore, Seoul, and Tokyo.
